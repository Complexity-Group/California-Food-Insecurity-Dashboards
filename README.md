# Food Insufficiency Trends Dashboard
## Overview

This dashboard visualizes trends in food insufficiency over time using three interactive charts. The data is displayed in millions of people and highlights how many individuals reported having sometimes not enough food, often not enough food, and the combined total of both categories.

The visualizations help identify changes in food insecurity levels and provide insight into long-term trends.

## Charts Included
### 1. Sometimes Not Enough Food

This chart displays the number of people (in millions) who reported sometimes not having enough food to eat.

#### Features:
- Monthly trend visualization
- Area chart representation
- Trend line overlay for easier pattern analysis
- Year labels for multi-year comparison
- Responsive design for desktop and mobile viewing

#### Purpose:
To track moderate levels of food insecurity and observe how conditions change over time.

### 2. Often Not Enough Food

This chart displays the number of people (in millions) who reported often not having enough food to eat.

#### Features:
- Monthly trend visualization
- Area chart representation
- Trend line overlay
- Year grouping for easier historical comparison
- Mobile-friendly responsive layout

#### Purpose:
To highlight severe food insecurity and identify periods where food access challenges become more significant.

### 3. Combined Food Insufficiency

#### This stacked area chart combines both categories:
- Sometimes Not Enough Food
- Often Not Enough Food

#### Features:
- Stacked area visualization
- Displays total food insufficiency levels
- Allows comparison between moderate and severe food insecurity
- Monthly and yearly trend tracking
- Responsive design for multiple screen sizes

#### Purpose:
To provide a complete picture of food insufficiency by showing the total population affected and the relative contribution of each category.

### Data Source
The dashboard retrieves data from a published Google Sheets CSV file and automatically updates when the underlying spreadsheet is modified.

Data values are measured in millions of persons.

### Key Insights
- Food insufficiency can be examined through both moderate ("sometimes") and severe ("often") categories.
- Trend lines help identify long-term patterns beyond month-to-month fluctuations.
- The stacked visualization provides a comprehensive view of the total population experiencing food insecurity.
- Comparing all three charts together allows users to better understand shifts in the severity and prevalence of food insufficiency over time.

### Technologies Used
- HTML5
- CSS3
- JavaScript
- Chart.js 4.4.1
- Google Sheets CSV Data Source

### Accessibility & Responsiveness
The dashboard is designed to:
- Work on desktop and mobile devices
- Maintain readability across screen sizes
- Display consistent labeling and year markers
- Present data in a clear and visually accessible format

This dashboard serves as a tool for monitoring food insecurity trends and supporting data-driven discussions around hunger and food access.
